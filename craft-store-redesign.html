Karnaluks redesign

Reimagining a craft supply shop to feel more like an inspiring studio — shifting from a product catalog into a place that supports inspiration, creativity, and reflection.

5 weeks (Feb–Mar 2026) · Figma · Lovable

CONTEXT

Emotional Design course. Karnaluks is a craft supply store with a wide range of hobbies; the physical shop is loved, but the site felt purely transactional and overwhelming.

The goal: rethink the website through emotional design — from catalog to a place that supports inspiration, creativity, and reflection.

WHAT I DESIGNED

More about the process

Four moves, from technical browsing to reflection — prototyped in Figma and Lovable so the tone could be tested early.

  • From technical to human. Conversational filters for desired outcomes and tactile preferences, not only composition.
  • Inspiration-first landing. Homepage leads with exploration; a short quiz routes to mood boards, fabrics, Pinterest, and peer projects.
  • Making inspiration achievable. Real customer work on product pages; what you can make with each material.
  • Room for reflection. Inspiration Diary — save boards, note projects, and see preferences accrue over time.

RESEARCH

The original site leaned on dense category cards and long product lists with little filtering. Organization assumed technical knowledge, which was hard for beginners; hierarchy and readability suffered.

An OOUX analysis mapped core flows and friction. I also ran a small embodied study (cooking — outside my comfort zone) to notice how starting points, play, and imagining an outcome shape motivation.

KEY ISSUES

  • Dense category cards with weak visual hierarchy.
  • Long product lists without useful filtering.
  • Technical terminology that alienates newcomers.
  • Poor scan and overall readability.

INSIGHTS

  • Beginners need a starting point for what to make.
  • The creative moment lives in play and adjustment.
  • Motivation grows from picturing the finished piece.
  • Appreciation makes the process stick in memory.

PROBLEM

  • The site functioned as a catalog, not a creative companion.
  • Taxonomy and copy assumed expert material knowledge.
  • Little help for people who feel stuck or uninspired.

KEY OUTCOMES

  • Conversational filtering — outcomes and feel, not only specs.
  • Inspiration-first landing instead of category walls.
  • Style quiz → personalised mood boards for stuck users.
  • Inspiration Diary — save ideas and track preferences over time.

KEY IDEA

A craft store is more than a list of products — it's a starting point for someone's creative journey.
